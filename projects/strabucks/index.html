PROJECT REPORT: CUSTOMER SPENDING PROJECTIONS AT STARBUCKS

CONCEPT

The Customer Spending Projections at Starbucks project aimed to analyze Starbucks’ sales data to understand customer spending patterns and provide insights for offering personalized coupons and recommendations. The project leveraged a dataset consisting of customer profiles, promotional offers, and transaction logs to gain insights into purchase decisions and offer influence.

DATASET OVERVIEW

The dataset comprised three files: profile.json, portfolio.json, and transcript.json. The profile file contained information about rewards program users, including gender, age, income, and membership details. The portfolio file provided details about the different types of promotional offers, such as reward, channels, difficulty, duration, and offer type. The transcript file captured event logs, including offer received, offer viewed, transaction, and offer completed, along with associated values and timestamps.

PROBLEM STATEMENT

The project aimed to answer three key questions:

  1. What is the spending pattern based on offer type and demographics?
  2. How can coupons/offers be recommended to current customers based on their spending patterns?
  3. How can coupons/offers be recommended to new customers?

APPROACH

The project involved the following steps:

  1. Data Exploration: Analyzing the dataset to understand the relationships between offer types, demographics, and spending patterns.
  2. Spending Pattern Analysis: Visualizing the purchase decisions based on different promotional offers and demographic factors.
  3. Recommender Systems: Implementing rank-based recommendation systems to filter potential users for viewing and redeeming existing or future offers. Utilizing user-user collaborative filtering methods to identify potential users specific to each offer.
  4. Recommendation for New Customers: Developing a machine learning approach to recommend offers to new customers based on their expected spending patterns. Setting rules for sending out coupons based on projected spending amounts.

ALGORITHMS USED

  • Rank-Based Recommendation System.
  • User-User Collaborative Filtering.
  • Machine Learning Models: XGBoost, RandomForest.

TOOLS USED

  • NumPy
  • Pandas
  • Scikit-Learn

RESULTS

  1. Spending Pattern Based on Offer Type and Demographics:

    • Discount offers were more popular in terms of completion, while BOGO offers had higher view counts.
    • Females and individuals with “Other” gender had a higher chance of redeeming offers.
    • Spending was observed to increase with age and income, with women tending to spend the most.

  2. Coupon/Offer Recommendations for Current Customers:

    • Rank-based recommendation systems and user-user collaborative filtering were used to identify potential users for offers based on their spending patterns.

  3. Coupon/Offer Recommendations for New Customers:

    • Machine learning approaches were employed to recommend offers to new customers based on their projected spending patterns. Specific rules were set to determine the appropriate coupon to send based on expected spending amounts.

CONCLUSION

The Customer Spending Projections at Starbucks project successfully analyzed Starbucks’ sales data to identify spending patterns based on offer type and demographics. Insights were provided for recommending coupons and offers to both current and new customers. The project showcased the potential of data science techniques, such as rank-based recommendations, collaborative filtering, and machine learning, in improving customer engagement and personalization strategies for Starbucks.
